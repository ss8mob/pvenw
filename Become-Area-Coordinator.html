Become An Area Coordinator

1. Program Overview & Purpose of the Role

The Palos Verdes Estates Neighborhood Watch program is a community-led safety initiative operating in partnership with the PVE Police Department. It is built on a network of engaged volunteers who serve as the eyes, ears, and communication backbone for public safety across the neighborhood.

The Area Coordinator is the cornerstone of this network. This individual manages the program across an entire designated area, overseeing a team of Block Captains, communicating with law enforcement, and fostering a culture of safety, trust, and civic participation.

Selecting the right person for this role is critical. This manual ensures the process is fair, transparent, and thorough.

2. Eligibility Requirements

All candidates must meet the following minimum criteria at the time of application:

Mandatory Requirements

  • Must be a current resident of Palos Verdes Estates (renter or homeowner).
  • Must reside within or adjacent to the designated coordination area.
  • Must be 18 years of age or older.
  • Must not have any felony convictions or active criminal matters.
  • Must be available to attend required meetings, trainings, and events.
  • Must have reliable access to email, phone, and basic digital communication tools.

Preferred Qualifications (not required, but weighted in scoring)

  • Prior experience in community organizing, civic leadership, or volunteer coordination.
  • Familiarity with the Neighborhood Watch program or similar public safety initiatives.
  • Established relationships with neighbors in the designated area.
  • Experience managing groups, committees, or volunteer teams.

3. Code of Conduct

The applicant must read, understand, and agree to abide by the Area Coordinator Code of Conduct in its entirety. Continued participation as a Neighborhood Watch Area Coordinator is conditional upon ongoing compliance with these standards.
