Social Media & Respectful Communication Guidelines

We strongly support everyone’s right to share their opinions and concerns. At the same time, we encourage all communication to remain respectful, constructive, and focused on the issue, so that we can maintain a positive and productive dialogue within our community.

By approaching these conversations thoughtfully, we help protect the spirit of cooperation and the positive reputation of the Neighborhood Watch.

When responding on social media, it is important to do so on a personal level, using ‘I’ statements rather than speaking as a Block Captain, Area Coordinator, or representative of the Neighborhood Watch.

The Safest Approach

The safest approach is to focus on issues, values, and solutions, not people. The emphasis is on keeping posts respectful, avoiding shaming or bullying, and being careful not to target identifiable individuals. Constructive criticism is less risky than personal attacks.

Safer Ways to Express Concerns

  • Talk about the policy or process, not the person.
  • Use “I” statements: “I’m concerned that…”.
  • Describe the impact on the community or the city.
  • Keep the tone calm and factual, even if you disagree strongly.

Helpful Phrases We Can Use

  • “There are ongoing concerns about this process, and I’d like to see a clearer plan.”
  • “I disagree with this approach because it does not meet community expectations.”
  • “Let’s focus on solutions that better serve residents.”

What to Avoid

  • Naming a neighbor, resident, or official in a critical post.
  • Using insults, sarcasm, mockery, or public shaming.
  • Reposting allegations about a person.
  • Turning a policy disagreement into a personal attack.
  • Making responses on behalf of the Neighborhood Watch.
  • Using Neighborhood Watch official role in social media discussions.

Practical Rule

  • If a sentence would feel uncomfortable being said face-to-face, it is probably too aggressive for social media.
  • The post should criticize the decision rather than the person.
