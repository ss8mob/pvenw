Become a Neighborhood Watch Block Captain

The block captain is a leadership role in our neighborhood.

Neighborhood watch block captains are volunteer community liaisons who connect local residents with law enforcement. Serving as a block captain is a rewarding experience and a great way to help make your neighborhood safer.

Block captains:

  • Are the eyes and ears for a block of houses.
  • Look for anything out of the ordinary that might need attention.
  • Carry out the Neighborhood Watch Program on a given street.
  • Are not vigilantes — they notify the police when necessary and do not try to act as police themselves.
  • Share information on crime trends and prevention tips with the neighborhood.

Are responsible for:

  • Organizing block meetings
  • Circulating crime alerts and safety tips
  • Updating emergency contact lists
  • Welcoming new neighbors to the block
This is a volunteer role and takes about 1 hour per month.

Key Responsibilities

  • Distribute information to members.
  • Recruit new members.
  • Provide current crime prevention information to members.
  • Update phone trees and email lists.
  • Announce, advertise, and lead meetings.
  • Receive crime and emergency alerts from local law enforcement and share via email trees or text groups.
  • Help build familiarity among neighbors so that unusual or suspicious activity is easily recognized and reported.
  • Organize at least one neighborhood meeting per year to discuss safety concerns.
  • Welcome new neighbors and hand out safety information.
  • Remind residents to report suspicious activity to emergency services.

Ideal Block Captain Candidate

An ideal block captain should possess these key traits:

Engaged & Accessible

Often at home, walks the dog, or works nearby.

Organized

Capable of maintaining a roster of names, phone numbers, and home addresses.

Good Communicator

Can easily relay crime alerts and important information via email, text, or a group chat.

Questions Asked and Answered

What is a block?
A block can be all the houses along one or more streets, or it may be a portion of the houses on a given street.
What are some of the duties of a block captain?
  • Creating a contact list for the block
  • Holding a block meeting to explain the program to their neighbors
  • Tracking incidents around the block and updating residents about any pertinent issues such as a break‐in
  • Coordinating street meets when needed
What are the benefits of having a block captain on our street?

It fosters communication and a close‐knit community on your block.

The time commitment is very small and the outcome can be extremely helpful. Block captains can alert residents to everything from break‐ins on their street to happy events going on.

The idea is to watch out for each other and keep communication open, which makes for a better community all around.
