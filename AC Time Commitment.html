Neighborhood Watch Program

Area Coordinator - Time Commitment

Neighborhood Watch area coordinators typically spend about 2 hours per month on their duties. The position acts as an informational liaison between local law enforcement, block captains, and residents.

Monthly Time Breakdown
  • Communication & Admin (1 hour):
    Forwarding crime alerts, distributing newsletters, or managing email.
  • Liaison & Reporting (30 mins):
    Communicating with your local police department to track trends.
  • Block Captain Check-ins (30 mins):
    Checking in with your block captains so they can pass information to their immediate blocks.
Tips for Efficiency
  • Delegate to Block Captains:
    Do not contact 100 residents yourself. Use a leadership hierarchy so you only communicate with 5-6 block captains.
  • Rely on Law Enforcement:
    Direct your community to call 911 for emergencies, and rely on your Police Department Liaison for accurate, and official crime data.
